# Pre-Event Outreach Template

Send 5-7 days before the event. Short. One ask.

Variables:
- `[FIRST_NAME]` - their first name
- `[EVENT_NAME]` - name of the event
- `[SPECIFIC_REASON]` - why you are reaching out to them specifically (their role, something they published, their company's relevant situation)
- `[YOUR_NAME]` - your name
- `[COMPANY_NAME]` - your company

---

## Version A: Via email (if you have it)

**Subject:** [EVENT_NAME] - worth connecting?

---

[FIRST_NAME],

Reaching out because I'll be at [EVENT_NAME] next week and [SPECIFIC_REASON - e.g., "your work in the MSP space is directly relevant to what we're building" or "saw your talk listed in the agenda"].

Would you be open to a quick coffee or 15-minute chat while we're both there?

[YOUR_NAME]
[COMPANY_NAME]

---

## Version B: Via LinkedIn

---

[FIRST_NAME] - heading to [EVENT_NAME] and wanted to connect ahead of time. [SPECIFIC_REASON]. Would be great to meet if you're around.

---

## Version C: Via LinkedIn (when you have a mutual connection)

---

[FIRST_NAME] - [MUTUAL_NAME] mentioned you'd be at [EVENT_NAME]. Would love to connect while we're both there. [SPECIFIC_REASON in one sentence].

---

## Notes on pre-event outreach

- Send early enough that they can respond and plan. 5-7 days is the window. Too early and they have not shifted focus to the event yet. Too late and schedules are full.
- The more specific your reason for reaching out, the higher the response rate. "I saw you're on the [PANEL_TOPIC] panel" beats "I noticed we're both attending."
- Your goal is to get on their radar before the event, not to pitch. Save the conversation for when you meet.
