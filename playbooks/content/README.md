# Content Playbook

A content machine built on your ICP. Produces consistent output that shortens sales cycles and generates inbound signals over time.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] F1 complete: ICP documented (determines what topics you write about)
- [ ] F3 complete: Message documented (determines how you write and what problems you address)

---

## What this playbook produces

- A core theme framework (1-2 topics you own)
- A 30-day publishing calendar with specific titles
- Long-form content (articles, LinkedIn posts, guides)
- Short-form content (LinkedIn carousels, punchy posts, X threads)
- A distribution system beyond just posting

---

## How content fits into GTM

Content does two things in this system:

1. **Warms up cold outreach.** When someone receives a cold email from you and recognizes your name because they read your post last week, reply rates increase. Content makes cold outreach feel warm.

2. **Generates inbound signals.** When prospects engage with your content (comment, share, DM you), they signal buying intent without asking for a proposal. These signals are worth more than a cold list.

Content is not a standalone channel for companies under 20 people. It is a force multiplier for cold email, events, and referral.

## Step 1: Define your core theme

Your theme is the intersection of:
- A problem your ICP cares about deeply (from F1 and F3)
- A perspective only you can deliver credibly

The theme is not your category. "GTM strategy" is not a theme. "Why referral-dependent B2B firms hit a ceiling at $2M and what to do about it" is a theme.

A strong theme:
- Is specific enough that your ICP recognizes themselves in it
- Is broad enough that you can write about it for 12 months
- Has a contrarian or counterintuitive angle that makes it worth sharing
- Is something you have actual evidence for (experience, data, client results)

**Exercise:** Write your theme as a single sentence that starts with "I write about..." or "My point of view is..."

If you struggle to write the sentence, your theme is not clear yet. Do not start publishing until it is.

---

## Step 2: Build a 30-day calendar

With your theme defined, map out 30 days of specific content ideas.

**Content mix:**

| Format | Frequency | Purpose |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Long-form article (600-1500 words) | 2x per month | Depth and SEO. Something worth bookmarking. |
| LinkedIn post (150-300 words) | 3x per week | Presence and signal generation. |
| Short-form hook post (1 paragraph) | 2x per week | Reach and shareability. |

**Calendar template:**

For each piece, define:
- Topic (specific, not vague)
- Format (article / LinkedIn post / hook)
- Core insight (the one thing the reader will take away)
- Connection to ICP's situation (why does this matter to your buyer right now?)
- CTA (what should the reader do next - reply, read another post, book a call?)

**Title formula for calendar planning:**

Structure titles to be searchable and specific:
- "[Number] reasons [ICP type] [problem they have]"
- "Why [common belief] is wrong for [ICP type]"
- "The [specific thing] that [ICP type] keeps missing"
- "What I've learned from [number] [ICP type] clients about [topic]"

---

## Step 3: Write and publish

**Long-form writing process:**
1. Write the insight in one paragraph first (what is the one thing you want the reader to take away?)
2. Build the argument around that insight
3. Use a real example (your own experience or a client situation - anonymized if needed)
4. End with a specific action the reader can take

**Short-form writing process:**
- Start with a hook: the first line must make someone stop scrolling. Start with a specific claim, a counterintuitive statement, or a question that names the reader's exact situation.
- Deliver the value in 3-5 short paragraphs or a numbered list
- End with a CTA: "What's yours?" / "Reply below" / "Read the full piece here"

## Step 4: Distribute

Publishing is not distribution. Distribution is how your content reaches your ICP beyond the people who already follow you.

**Distribution system:**

1. **Post natively** on LinkedIn (and X if relevant to your ICP)
2. **Send to your email list** (even if it's small - 50 people who opted in are worth more than 5,000 followers)
3. **Share in communities** where your ICP is active (Slack groups, forums, newsletters that accept submissions)
4. **Repurpose:** every long-form article generates 3-5 LinkedIn posts. Every LinkedIn post can be a cold email PS line or a DM opener.
5. **Reference in outreach:** when doing cold email or LinkedIn outreach, link to a relevant piece as proof of expertise

## Metrics to track

| Metric | What it tells you |
|--------|------------------|
| Impressions per post | Is this topic resonating with the algorithm? |
| Comments per post | Are you saying something worth responding to? |
| Profile visits from content | Is content driving discovery? |
| Inbound inquiries | Is content converting to conversations? |
| Content mentions in outreach replies | Is content warming up cold contacts? |
