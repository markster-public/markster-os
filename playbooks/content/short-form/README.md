# Short-Form Content

LinkedIn posts, X threads, and punchy single-insight pieces.

Short-form content generates presence and signals. It keeps you visible to people who already know you and makes you discoverable to people who do not.

---

## What works on LinkedIn (for B2B)

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards:
- Early engagement (comments in the first 60 minutes matter most)
- Dwell time (posts people read all the way through)
- Comments over likes (comments signal real engagement)
- Native content (no outbound links in the main body - put them in comments)

**What performs consistently:**
- Personal story with a specific lesson (not motivational - specific and practical)
- Counterintuitive claim with a logical argument
- Short numbered list with a non-obvious insight in each item
- A question that your ICP would want to answer publicly

**What does not perform:**
- Celebrating a work anniversary or award (your network celebrates, strangers scroll past)
- Vague inspirational statements
- Screenshot of a tweet with "thoughts?"
- Long paragraphs without formatting

---

## Short-form writing process

1. Start with the hook (first 1-2 lines visible before "see more")
2. Deliver the value in 200-400 words
3. End with one CTA or a question that invites replies

The hook is everything. If the first two lines do not stop the scroll, nothing else matters.

**Hook formulas that work:**
- "Most [ICP type] I know are doing [thing] wrong." - Then explain why.
- "I used to believe [common belief]. Then [specific experience] changed my mind."
- "Unpopular opinion: [specific contrarian claim]."
- "[Number] ways I [achieved result] without [sacrifice]."
- "The question that changed how I think about [topic]:" - Then ask it.

---

## Posting cadence

For consistent compound growth: 3-5 posts per week on LinkedIn.

If you are starting from zero, focus on quality over frequency. Two high-quality posts per week beats five mediocre ones.
