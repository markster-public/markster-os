# LinkedIn Post Template

Short-form posts for LinkedIn. Under 400 words. Built to stop the scroll.

---

## Format A: Counterintuitive claim

Use for: sharing a perspective that goes against common advice in your category.

---

[CONTRARIAN CLAIM in one sentence. Something your ICP would react to - agree or disagree, but not ignore.]

Here is why I think that:

[Sentence 1: context or setup]
[Sentence 2: the specific evidence or experience]
[Sentence 3: the implication]

Most [ICP type] I talk to do [common behavior]. The result is [what typically happens].

When you [do the alternative instead], [what changes].

[Optional: one client example with specific detail - no name needed, but be specific about situation and result]

What is your take? [specific question that invites replies]

---

## Format B: Numbered list

Use for: practical lessons or observations that stand alone as individual insights.

---

[NUMBER] things I've learned from working with [ICP type]:

1. [Specific insight - not generic advice]
2. [Specific insight]
3. [Specific insight]
4. [Specific insight]
5. [Specific insight]

The one that surprises people most: [elaborate on the most counterintuitive item in 2-3 sentences]

[Optional CTA: "Anything on this list match your experience?"]

---

## Format C: Personal story with a lesson

Use for: building trust through specific, honest experience sharing.

---

[Scene-setting first sentence. Specific, not vague. "In 2023, I had a client who refused to send cold email because..." not "I've learned a lot from my clients..."]

[What happened - 2-3 sentences. Specific details. What was at stake.]

[What you expected vs. what actually happened]

[The lesson in one sentence - state it directly]

[One practical application for the reader - what can they do with this?]

[Optional: question to invite replies]

---

## Format D: Short hook post

Use for: quick wins, observations, provocations. Under 150 words. High frequency.

---

[1-2 line hook - the entire post is almost this short]

[3-5 lines of substance - the punchline or the elaboration]

[One line CTA or closing thought]

---

## Formatting rules for LinkedIn

- Line breaks between every paragraph (not bullet points)
- Maximum 3-4 sentences per paragraph
- Bold sparingly - use it for the one phrase that is the crux of the post
- No links in the body text - LinkedIn suppresses reach for posts with outbound links. Put links in the first comment.
- Write "2-3 sentences" not "two to three sentences" - numbers read faster in a feed

---

## Before posting: the scroll test

Read your post as if you are scrolling through LinkedIn on your phone. Ask:
- Does the first line make me want to read the second line?
- Is every sentence earning its place?
- Would I share this if a colleague wrote it?

If not, rewrite the first line first. Everything else follows.
