# Long-Form Content

Long-form content for B2B audiences: articles, guides, case studies, and deep-dive posts.

Long-form has two jobs: establish credibility with buyers who research before reaching out, and generate SEO-indexed content that surfaces in search results.

---

## What counts as long-form

- Articles: 600-1500 words. One clear argument. Published on LinkedIn or your website.
- Guides: 1500-3000 words. Reference material. Designed to be saved and shared.
- Case studies: 400-800 words. One client situation, one result, specific details. Not "we helped a company grow revenue."
- Deep-dive posts: 500-1000 words. A single insight explored thoroughly.

---

## Writing process

**Step 1: Start with the insight**

Every long-form piece should have one core insight - the thing you believe that most people in your category do not say out loud, or the thing you have learned from working with clients that is not obvious from the outside.

Write the insight in one sentence before you write anything else. If you cannot write the insight in one sentence, you do not know what you are trying to say yet.

**Step 2: Build the structure**

A reliable structure for B2B long-form:
- Open with the problem or situation (not a generic industry trend - a specific scenario the reader recognizes)
- State what most people think or do about it
- Introduce the counterpoint or new angle
- Back it up with evidence (your experience, client data, or a logical argument)
- End with a practical takeaway

**Step 3: Write for skimmers first**

Most readers will skim before they read. Use:
- Bold subheadings that communicate the structure
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Pull quotes or highlighted key sentences
- A clear opening paragraph that earns the scroll

**Step 4: Add one CTA**

Every piece should have one action you want the reader to take. Follow-up article, reply to you, book a call, or share with someone. One CTA, clearly stated.

---

## SEO guidance

If you are writing for SEO (website articles), include the target keyword in:
- The title
- The first 100 words
- One subheading
- The meta description (if your CMS supports it)

Do not stuff keywords. Write for the reader first. Search engines reward content that earns engagement.
