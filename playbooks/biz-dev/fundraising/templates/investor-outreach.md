# Investor Outreach Templates

Cold and warm outreach for investor introductions. Keep it short. Lead with traction.

---

## Cold Email - Lead with traction

**Subject:** [COMPANY] - [one metric] in [timeframe]

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

[COMPANY] [what you do in one sentence - problem and solution].

Traction: [your strongest number - ARR, customers, growth rate, retention]. [One supporting data point if you have it].

Raising [amount] to [specific milestone this raise achieves].

[RELEVANT_REASON you are reaching out to them specifically - portfolio company, sector focus, tweet or post they wrote].

Worth a 15-minute call?

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Cold Email - Lead with the thesis

Use when: you have less traction but a compelling market timing argument.

**Subject:** [COMPANY] - [market timing or insight]

---

[INVESTOR_FIRST_NAME],

[One sentence that frames the market change or opportunity - why this, why now].

[COMPANY] [what you do in one sentence].

We are [3-6 months] in with [early traction or validation - be honest, not inflated].

Raising [amount] to [specific next milestone].

Saw your [post / portfolio / comment about sector]. Thought this might be worth 15 minutes.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Warm Intro Request (to send to a mutual connection)

---

[MUTUAL_FIRST_NAME],

Quick ask: I am building relationships with investors in [sector] as we approach our raise, and I noticed you know [INVESTOR_NAME] at [FUND].

If you think there is a fit, would you be willing to make an intro? Happy to send you a quick blurb to forward if that is easier.

Not urgent - only if you genuinely think it is a good match.

[YOUR_NAME]

---

## Blurb for mutual to forward

---

[MUTUAL_NAME] asked me to forward this introduction.

[YOUR_NAME] is the [title] of [COMPANY] - [one sentence on what you do and for whom].

Traction: [top metric]. Raising [amount]. [One sentence on why this fund specifically].

[YOUR_NAME]'s contact: [email]

---

## Notes on investor outreach

- Keep cold emails under 100 words. Investors receive hundreds per week. Long emails go unread.
- Lead with the number, not the vision. Vision is in the meeting.
- No deck in the first cold email. If they are interested, they will ask.
- If you have traction, that is your subject line. If you do not have traction yet, your subject line is the market insight.
- Personalization is required. "I invest in B2B SaaS" is not a reason to reach out to someone - find something specific.
