# Fundraising Playbook

A structured approach to building an investor pipeline and moving from first contact to term sheet.

This playbook is designed for early-stage and growth-stage companies raising their first institutional or pre-institutional round.

---

## Prerequisites

- [ ] F1 complete: Know your company's investor ICP (not all investors - the right type for your stage, sector, and check size)
- [ ] F2 complete: Your offer to investors - the investment thesis, traction, and outcome narrative

---

## What this playbook produces

- A categorized investor pipeline (100-200 target contacts)
- A warm and cold outreach sequence
- A deck outline that follows investor expectations
- A meeting tracking and follow-up system
- A diligence readiness checklist

---

## Step 1: Define your investor ICP

Not all capital is the same. Define who you are raising from before building a list.

**Investor characteristics to define:**

- Stage fit: pre-seed, seed, Series A? Are they writing checks at your traction level?
- Sector fit: do they invest in your category? (Not just "SaaS" or "B2B" - specifically your niche)
- Check size: does your raise match their typical check range?
- Value-add: do they bring relevant portfolio companies, introductions, or operating experience?
- Geography: do they invest in your market?

**Sources for building your list:**
- Crunchbase and AngelList filtered by stage, sector, and recent activity
- LinkedIn search for partners at funds with portfolio companies similar to yours
- Portfolio companies of target funds - who invested in them?
- Founder communities and warm intros from other founders

**Categorize into tiers:**

- Tier 1 (10-20 contacts): Ideal investors - right stage, sector, check size, known for value-add. Warm intro required.
- Tier 2 (30-50 contacts): Good fit but lower priority or no warm path. Pursue in parallel with Tier 1.
- Tier 3 (50-100 contacts): Possible fit, low conviction. Cold outreach only if Tier 1-2 pipeline is thin.

---

## Step 2: Outreach

**Principle: warm intro first, cold email second.**

A warm intro from a portfolio founder or mutual connection converts at 5-10x the rate of a cold email to an investor. Spend time mapping intro paths before sending cold outreach.

**Warm intro path:**
- Identify mutual connections (LinkedIn second-degree connections to the partner)
- Ask your existing network: "Do you know anyone at [Fund]? I'm building relationships with firms in [sector]."
- Ask early customers, advisors, or angels for introductions to investors they know

**Cold outreach (for Tier 2-3 when no warm path exists):**

Use [investor-outreach.md](templates/investor-outreach.md) template.

Key rules for investor cold email:
- One paragraph max. They read hundreds of emails per week.
- Lead with the traction number, not the vision.
- Make the ask specific: "15-minute intro call" not "looking to connect."
- No deck in the first email. Get a response first.

---

## Step 3: The pitch meeting

**Structure:**

- Minutes 0-5: Why you? (Founder credibility and why you uniquely are solving this)
- Minutes 5-15: The market and the problem (size, urgency, why now)
- Minutes 15-25: The product and traction (what you built, what it does, numbers)
- Minutes 25-35: The ask (how much, what it buys, what the milestones are)
- Minutes 35-45: Questions

**What they are evaluating:**
1. Is this a real market?
2. Is this team capable of winning it?
3. Is the traction believable?
4. Is the investment thesis coherent?

**Deck structure:**

1. Cover: company name, one-liner, date
2. Problem: the specific situation your customer is in, why it matters
3. Solution: what you built, one sentence
4. Why now: the market timing argument
5. Traction: the most compelling numbers you have
6. Product: how it works (screenshots, not diagrams)
7. Business model: how you make money
8. Market size: TAM / SAM / SOM (with logic, not just a number)
9. Team: the two or three things about your background that make you the right people for this
10. The ask: raise amount, use of funds, milestones

---

## Step 4: Follow-up and close

**After every meeting: follow up within 24 hours.**

Send a short email referencing the meeting, the one thing that resonated most (what did they respond to?), and the next step.

Use [follow-up.md](templates/follow-up.md).

**Managing the process:**

- Track every investor in your CRM with: stage (intro / meeting / diligence / term sheet / passed), last contact date, next action
- Set weekly tasks for every active investor relationship
- Create momentum by being transparent about interest: "We have two investors completing diligence this week" signals urgency without pressure

**When they ask for more time:**

Most passes are not final. They are "not yet" or "not enough traction." Ask: "What would need to be true in the next 90 days for this to be an easier yes?"

Their answer is your roadmap.

---

## Diligence readiness checklist

Before any serious investor conversation:
- [ ] Financial model (18-month projection with assumptions)
- [ ] Cap table (clean, in a shareholder registry)
- [ ] Key contracts (customer agreements, vendor agreements)
- [ ] IP ownership documentation
- [ ] Incorporation documents and any existing convertible notes or SAFEs
- [ ] Reference customers you can introduce (3 minimum)
