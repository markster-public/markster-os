# Segment Profile: HVAC / Trades

## Definition

HVAC contractors, plumbers, electricians, and general contractors provide skilled trade services to residential and commercial customers. They are highly operational, seasonally driven, and almost universally dependent on lead platforms (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack) or word of mouth. They are plain-spoken, time-poor, and deeply skeptical of marketing vendors.

---

## Why This Segment

- **Strong proof-of-concept vertical.** The operational model (local service, truck-based, seasonal demand) is highly documented with real case studies and benchmarks.
- **Massive pain with lead platforms.** Almost every trades owner is spending $3-10K/month on Angi or HomeAdvisor and getting burned by low-quality, shared leads. This is active, felt pain.
- **Low content sophistication = fast wins.** Any consistent, local, authentic content outperforms the zero-bar competition. Results come quickly.
- **Owner-operated with full decision authority.** No committee, no approval chain. If you convince the owner, you close.

---

## Specific Pain Points

1. **Lead platform dependency and burnout.** Spending thousands per month on Angi/HomeAdvisor for leads that are shared with 3-5 competitors, half of whom do not answer the phone. Margins are eroding.
2. **Seasonal demand volatility.** HVAC lives and dies by summer heat and winter breakdowns. Off-season revenue is thin and unpredictable without a proactive pipeline.
3. **No differentiation.** Every HVAC company website says "fast, reliable, affordable." There is no content, no story, no reason to choose them over the next result in Google.
4. **Owner doing everything.** The owner is often on the truck, managing crews, doing estimates, and running the office. There is no time or capacity to build a marketing system.
5. **Bad vendor experiences.** Most trades owners have paid for SEO, social media management, or a website redesign and seen zero results. Trust in marketing vendors is extremely low.

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|

| Age range | 35-55 |
| Background | Came up through the trade, became a technician, eventually started their own shop |
| Buying mode | Highly skeptical. Needs to see proof from someone in the same trade. Does not respond to theory. |
| Fear | Wasting money on marketing that does not produce jobs |
| Desire | More jobs booked without paying Angi another dollar |

---

## Buying Trigger Events

- Complaining in trades groups or to peers about Angi/HomeAdvisor costs and quality
- Hiring a marketing person and not knowing what to have them do
- A competitor suddenly showing up in Google results or on social media
- A slow month after canceling a lead platform subscription
- Off-season revenue dropping to a level that forces the conversation

## Tone and Messaging Guidance

- Plain-spoken, concrete, no jargon. Trades owners hate marketing speak.
- Use their language: "jobs" not "deals," "customers" not "clients," "shop" not "office."
- Keep it short. These are busy people running crews, not sitting at desks reading long emails.
- Lead with real numbers from a real trades business -- not concepts.
- Do NOT lead with AI. Lead with outcomes and real numbers.
- Offer to meet locally or connect informally -- lower stakes than a formal sales call.
- Local presence matters. If you are in their market, say so.

**Vocabulary to use:** "jobs," "bookings," "customers," "shop," "crew," "appointments," "leads," "referrals"
**Vocabulary to avoid:** "pipeline," "ICP," "GTM," "thought leadership," "content strategy," "brand awareness," "funnel"

## Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I already use HomeAdvisor/Angi" | "Most HVAC guys spend $5-10K/month on those leads. Half do not pick up. There is a better way." |
| "I get all my work from referrals" | "That is great until it slows down. What is your backup plan for a slow month?" |
| "I do not need marketing" | "You might not. But HVAC owners who built a system are booking twice as many jobs without spending more." |
| "I am not tech-savvy" | "You do not need to be. You run your business, we run the system. You approve what goes out." |

## Seasonal Timing

- **Summer (HVAC cooling season):** All-consuming. Brief outreach OK, but owners are slammed. Keep messages very short.
- **Fall:** Best window for HVAC. Post-summer, pre-heating season. Owners have revenue and headspace.
- **Winter:** Heating emergency season. Similar to summer -- brief only.
- **Spring:** Planning season. Good time for longer conversations and contract starts.

For plumbing and electrical: less seasonal variation. Year-round timing applies.
