# F1: Ideal Customer Profile

**The question:** Who exactly are you selling to - and why do they buy now?

---

## Why this step matters

An ICP is not a target market. A target market is "B2B companies with 10-50 employees." An ICP is "a CPA firm owner with 5-15 staff in a major metro, growing by referral, who recently lost a key client and is realizing his pipeline is invisible to anyone who doesn't already know him."

The difference is the buying trigger. Two companies with identical firmographics can have completely different purchase likelihood based on what is happening inside the business right now.

Skip this step and you will write messages that resonate with no one, build lists that don't convert, and run campaigns that get replies like "not a good fit right now" - which means you were talking to the right company at the wrong moment.

---

## Three qualifier layers

Every strong ICP has three layers. You need all three.

### Layer 1: Firmographic
The measurable, filterable facts about the company.

Answer these:
- What industry or vertical?
- What revenue range?
- What headcount range?
- What geography?
- What business model (B2B service, SaaS, product, etc.)?
- What stage (startup, growth, established, scaling)?

### Layer 2: Behavioral
What they are already doing that signals readiness.

Answer these:
- What do they do when they are stuck? (Google, hire, referral, LinkedIn?)
- How do they currently acquire clients? (Referral, outbound, events, content?)
- What tools or systems are they already using?
- What does their buying process look like? (Solo decision vs. committee?)
- How long does a purchase typically take from first conversation to signed contract?
- What have they tried before to solve this problem?

### Layer 3: Situational
What is happening right now that makes them a buyer.

This is the most important layer and the one most companies skip. The situation is what triggers the purchase.

Answer these:
- What recent event makes this problem urgent? (Lost a key account, missed a growth target, hired a new person, pivoted the offering?)
- What changes in their environment are making the old way unsustainable? (AI disruption, new competitor, regulation change, market shift?)
- What internal pain are they feeling that they have not publicly admitted yet?
- At what point does the pain get bad enough to buy a solution?

---

## Decision-maker profile

Once you know the company, identify the person.

- What is their title or role?
- How old are they typically?
- What is their professional background?
- What do they care about in their role?
- What keeps them up at night professionally?
- What does success look like for them personally, not just for the company?
- Who else influences the purchase decision?
- What language do they use when describing this problem? (Find their words, not yours)

---

## Pain trigger identification

The trigger is the moment the ICP moves from "aware of the problem" to "actively looking for a solution."

Common trigger types:
- Loss event (lost a client, missed a target, key person left)
- Growth milestone (just hit a threshold that exposed a gap)
- Public commitment (launched a new offering, announced a hire, published a goal)
- Competitive pressure (competitor is visible and winning deals they should be winning)
- Regulatory or market shift (something external changed the status quo)
- Internal decision (just got budget, just approved a plan, just set a new goal)

Identify your trigger: what specific event moves your ICP from passive to active?

---

## Questions to answer in writing

Complete these before moving to F2. Use your own words. Do not use marketing language.

1. Describe your best current customer in one paragraph - not what they do, but who they are.
2. What did they say to you (or to themselves) the week before they decided to buy?
3. What would have to be true about their situation for them to say "I can't afford NOT to do this"?
4. What does a bad-fit customer look like? (Same industry, wrong situation?)
5. If you could clone one customer 20 times, who would it be and why?
