# ScaleOS Stage 1: Starting from Zero
## Markster OS Implementation Guide

---

## What Stage 1 Is

Stage 1 is the founder's proving ground - the phase where clarity is built through conversation, not construction. Your job is not to build infrastructure. It is to gather signal. F1 (Identity) and F2 (Message) inside the GOD Engine cannot be installed from a desk - they get installed through real conversations with real buyers. Stage 1 ends when you can describe who buys from you, why they buy, and how you win - without hedging.

---

## Entry Criteria: What "Stage 1" Means in Measurable Terms

You are in Stage 1 if ALL of the following are true:

- You have fewer than 3 paying clients with a repeatable close pattern
- You cannot state your ICP in 30 seconds without saying "it depends"
- You cannot describe the trigger event that turns a fit company into a buyer NOW
- You have not sent 50+ manual outreach messages and analyzed what landed
- You cannot name the 1-2 message angles that consistently produce replies
- Your deal pattern (trigger > outreach > conversation > proposal > close) is not yet documented

If two or more are true, you are in Stage 1. Start here.

---

## Week 1 Actions (Days 1-7)

**Days 1-3: Hypothesis Construction**

1. Write a one-page ICP hypothesis. Answer four questions only: Who is the buyer (company type, size, role)? What specific pain do they feel? What outcome do you deliver? Why you over alternatives?
2. Pull your last 3-5 best clients or strongest professional relationships. List what they have in common - industry, size, trigger event.
3. Pick ONE segment. Not "B2B companies." Not "SaaS or agencies." One vertical and one company size band. Examples: marketing agencies with 10-50 employees; accounting firms under $3M revenue.
4. Write your Minimum Viable Offer (MVO) in two sentences. If it requires qualifications or "it depends" - tighten the scope until it fits two sentences. A testable offer has: a named outcome, a named buyer, a delivery mechanism, and a price signal.

**Days 3-7: Discovery Conversations**

5. Identify 5-10 warm contacts - past clients, former colleagues, referral sources - who match or are adjacent to your ICP. Book brief 20-minute calls.
6. Conduct those calls as intelligence operations, not pitches. Ask: What is the biggest bottleneck with X? How are you currently solving it? What would need to be true for you to change?
7. Capture voice-of-customer language verbatim. The phrases they use to describe their pain are your future message copy.
8. Build a 50-account target list manually in Airtable or Google Sheets - not a CRM yet. Filter by vertical, company size, and decision-maker title using LinkedIn or Apollo.
9. Log all learnings daily in a running document: patterns you noticed, objections heard, phrases that got a reaction.

**What you do NOT do in Week 1:** buy automation tools, write email sequences, build a website, create a deck, set up a calendar booking link for cold traffic, or define your delivery process.

---

## Days 8-30 Actions

**Network Mining (Days 7-14)**

10. Message 20-30 warm contacts - past clients, colleagues, connectors. Do not pitch. Ask: "I'm working with [ICP type] on [specific pain]. Do you know anyone fitting that profile who might benefit from a quick conversation?" A referral from a trusted contact is worth 10 cold outreaches.

**Manual LinkedIn Outreach (Days 10-30)**

11. Identify 50 ICP-matching accounts from your target list. Write the first 20-30 messages by hand - not templates. Research each person: their role, what the company is doing, what trigger applies.
12. Send connection requests with a specific, human opener. Wait for acceptance before following up. Do not pitch on the connection request.
13. Test 2-3 different message angles against similar personas. Track which angle produces replies. One variable changes per iteration - list, message, or angle. Never all at once.
14. Every positive reply converts into a 20-30 minute discovery call. Book it within 24 hours of the reply.

**Discovery Calls (Days 14-30)**

15. Run each call as structured listening. Agenda: What is the biggest challenge with X? How are you solving it today? What have you tried that has not worked? What would success look like in 90 days?
16. Update your objection log after every call. Patterns emerge quickly.
17. Update your ICP hypothesis as you learn. The ICP sharpens meaningfully every 10-20 conversations.

**Hard rule:** no automation until you have sent 50+ manual messages and held 10+ discovery calls. Automating before validation means scaling the wrong message to the wrong people.

---

## Days 31-90 Actions

**Weeks 5-6: Message Consolidation**

18. By this point you should have data from 30-50 outreach messages and 5-10 discovery calls. Review your tracking log: which message angle produced the most positive replies? Lock in that angle as your primary.
19. Expand your account list to 100-150 companies. Continue manual outreach at 15-25 messages per day.
20. Refine your MVO based on language from discovery calls. Buyers will tell you how to position the offer - use their words.

**Weeks 7-12: First Clients**

21. Convert the strongest discovery call relationships into proposals. Keep proposals simple: outcome, timeline, price, next step. One page maximum.
22. Close your first 2-3 clients. Deliver carefully - proof points from early delivery become your future positioning.
23. Document the full deal pattern in writing: trigger > outreach > conversation > proposal > close. This becomes the F2 foundation for Stage 2.
24. Track conversion rates at every stage: outreach sent, positive replies, calls booked, proposals sent, deals closed.
25. By Day 90, target: 20+ discovery conversations, 200+ manual messages sent, 2-3 paying clients or strong verbal commitments.

## Weekly Routine at Stage 1

**Weeks 1-2 (Foundation - primary constraint is clarity)**

- Daily: 2-3 hours on ICP hypothesis and offer drafting
- Daily: 1-2 hours on warm network calls
- Daily: 1 hour building target list manually
- Daily: 30 minutes logging learnings
- No outbound. No pitching. Only listening and documenting.

**Weeks 3-6 (Manual Outreach - primary constraint is message-market fit)**

- Daily: 1.5-2 hours writing LinkedIn outreach manually (10-20 new contacts)
- Daily: 1 hour managing replies and follow-ups (same-day response)
- Daily: 1-2 hours on discovery calls booked from replies
- Daily: 30 minutes on message iteration and tracking update

**Friday checkpoint every week:**
- How many messages sent this week?
- How many positive replies?
- How many calls booked?
- Which message angle performed best?
- Did I speak to at least 3 actual ICP-match people this week?

**Weeks 7-12 (First Clients - primary constraint begins shifting to capacity)**

- Daily: 1-1.5 hours outreach (message is now proven, faster to write)
- Daily: 1.5-2 hours on discovery, follow-up, and close calls
- Daily: 1-2 hours on client delivery
- Daily: 30 minutes tracking pipeline conversion rates

## Exit Criteria: How You Know You Are Ready for Stage 2

Stage 2 begins when the bottleneck shifts from clarity to capacity. You are no longer guessing what works - you are running out of hours to do more of it.

All of the following should be true before moving to Stage 2:

| Signal | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| Discovery conversations held | 20+ with real ICP-match companies |
| Paying clients or signed deals | 2-3 minimum (or strong verbal commitments) |
| Positive reply rate on outreach | 5-10% consistently |
| ICP description | Stated in 30 seconds without hedging |
| Deal pattern | Documented: trigger - outreach - conversation - proposal - close |
| Message-market fit | You know which 1-2 angles produce replies |
| Manual ceiling | You are losing deals from lack of hours, not lack of clarity |

Soft signals that also indicate readiness:
- You can write a cold message in under 15 minutes
- You know the 3-5 most common objections and your responses to them
- You have delivered at least one project and can describe the outcome
- You have stopped guessing which segment to target

Do not move to Stage 2 before these signals are present. Hiring or building a system for an unvalidated message is the most expensive mistake in early-stage B2B sales.

## Common Failure Modes

**1. Targeting too broad (most common)**
Testing multiple industries, company sizes, or buyer types at once. The result is zero interpretable signal - nothing is isolated, so you cannot tell what is working. Fix: one segment, validate it, then expand.

**2. Building before validating**
Spending Weeks 1-6 creating a website, proposal template, onboarding process, and automation stack before a single prospect has confirmed interest. The offer has never been tested. This creates sunk-cost pressure to stick with an unvalidated direction.

**3. Validating with non-ICP people**
Talking to people who look like the ICP but are not - wrong company size, no budget authority, different internal context. A VP at a 5-person team and a VP managing 60 people share a title and nothing else. This produces confident wrong conclusions.

**4. Volume before validation**
Defaulting to mass outreach before the message has been tested manually. Buying 1,000 contacts and running sequences before knowing what message lands burns domains, burns goodwill with the ICP, and produces no usable signal on what to fix.

**5. Changing too many variables at once**
Adjusting list, message, subject line, and channel simultaneously. No attribution is possible. Treat Stage 1 outreach as a controlled experiment: one variable changes per iteration.

**6. Infrastructure without signal**
Buying an outreach tool before knowing what message to put in it. The GOD Engine principle applies here: systems amplify what already works. At Stage 1, nothing is proven yet. Infrastructure without signal scales noise.
