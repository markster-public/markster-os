# Pricing Perception Prompt

**Purpose:** Understand how your ICP thinks about price for solutions in your category. What do they compare your price against? What makes a price feel justified vs. high? What price anchors do they carry into the conversation?

---

## Variables to fill in

- `[ICP_DESCRIPTION]` - your ICP (from F1)
- `[SOLUTION_CATEGORY]` - what your solution helps with
- `[PRICE_RANGE]` - your current or planned pricing

---

## Research prompt

---

I am researching pricing perception for [ICP_DESCRIPTION] buying [SOLUTION_CATEGORY] at approximately [PRICE_RANGE].

**1. Price anchors**
What do these buyers compare your price against when evaluating whether it is reasonable or high?
- What alternative spending (hiring, agency retainers, tools) serves as a mental benchmark?
- What does the status quo (not solving the problem) cost them in time or money?
- What does a comparable investment in their business look like? (Other decisions in the same size range they make regularly)

**2. Value perception drivers**
What factors make a price feel justified to this buyer type?
- Speed of result (faster = more valuable)
- Certainty of result (guaranteed = reduces perceived risk)
- Effort reduction (less work for them = worth more)
- Specificity to their situation (industry-specific vs. generic)
- Proof from similar companies
- Who they hear about you from (referral vs. cold)

**3. Price resistance triggers**
What triggers sticker shock or price resistance in this buyer type?
- Upfront payment vs. monthly
- Lack of visibility into what they are getting
- No success story from a comparable company
- Ambiguity about what happens if it does not work
- Comparison to cheaper alternatives they are aware of

**4. Willingness to pay indicators**
What characteristics of a buyer (or their situation) correlate with higher willingness to pay?
- Stage of business
- Urgency of the problem
- Size of the problem (revenue affected, time consumed)
- Previous purchase behavior in this category
