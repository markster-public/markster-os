# First 60 Seconds Prompt

**Purpose:** Develop what to say in the first 60 seconds of a conversation with your ICP - whether at an event, on a cold call, in a discovery call introduction, or when someone asks "what do you do?" - to make them lean in rather than give a polite nod and move on.

---

## Variables to fill in

- `[ICP_DESCRIPTION]` - your ICP (from F1)
- `[YOUR_OUTCOME_STATEMENT]` - your F2 outcome statement
- `[TOP_PROBLEM]` - the top problem your ICP has (from F1 and F3)
- `[PROOF_POINT]` - your most specific proof point (from F3)
- `[CONTEXT]` - the context where this will be used (event intro, cold call, discovery call, LinkedIn connection call)

---

## Research prompt

---

I need to develop what to say in the first 60 seconds when meeting [ICP_DESCRIPTION] in the context of [CONTEXT].

My outcome: [YOUR_OUTCOME_STATEMENT]
Their top problem: [TOP_PROBLEM]
My proof: [PROOF_POINT]

**1. The "what do you do" answer**
Most professional service answers to "what do you do" are forgettable. Generate 3 versions of an answer that:
- Opens with the problem, not the solution
- Is specific enough that the right person thinks "that's me"
- Is interesting enough that the right person asks a follow-up question
- Is short enough to say in under 15 seconds

**2. The opening question**
If I am the one initiating the conversation (event, cold call), what question or statement should open it that:
- Feels natural rather than scripted
- Invites them to talk about their situation (not about me)
- Sets up a natural path toward discussing the problem I solve if it is relevant to them

**3. The interest signal question**
After 2-3 exchanges, I want to know quickly whether this person has the problem I solve. What is the one question I can ask that:
- Does not feel like a qualification question
- Gets them talking about the specific situation I need to understand
- Either opens the door to a real conversation or tells me clearly this is not the right fit

**4. The transition to next step**
If the signal is positive (they have the problem), what do I say to get agreement on a next step in a natural way that does not feel like a pitch?

---

## Output format

```
FIRST 60 SECONDS - [DATE]

Context: [event / cold call / discovery intro / etc.]
ICP: [description]

"What do you do" answers (pick one to use):
Option A: [version 1 - under 15 seconds]
Option B: [version 2 - different angle]
Option C: [version 3 - most direct]

Opening question (for when I initiate):
"[question]"

Interest signal question:
"[question to understand their situation]"

Transition to next step (when signal is positive):
"[natural transition]"

Test: would you say this to a stranger at a cocktail party without feeling awkward? Yes / No
If no: rewrite until yes.
```

Feed findings into: events playbook (during-event conversation framework) and discovery call prep (opening framing).
